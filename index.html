About me

Hi there! I’m Mitchelle Ngare, a sophomore currently pursuing Computer Science and Digital Art and a minor in Economics at Dartmouth College. My passion for technology and creativity fuels my work, and I am constantly exploring how these fields intersect. With a background in coding and web design, I thrive on solving complex problems through innovative solutions. I also enjoy delving into digital arts, focusing on character modeling and animation. My goal is to blend technical expertise with artistic expression, building projects that are not only functional but visually compelling. Thanks for visiting my portfolio—feel free to explore my work and get in touch!

Room Project

For my room project, I designed a clean modern house featuring big windows that allow for ample natural light and create an open, airy atmosphere. The design emphasizes simplicity and functionality, with sleek lines and minimalist decor. The key feature of the design is the bold windows, which serve as both a functional and aesthetic element, offering expansive views and enhancing the connection between the interior and exterior.

The design choices for the house were inspired by a desire to create a serene and comfortable living space that seamlessly blends with its natural surroundings


Abstract Project

This abstract design features concentric circles in a gradient of teal to blue, creating a sense of depth and movement. The swirling pattern evokes the flow of energy or time, drawing the viewer’s eye toward the center.

Biped Project

For my animation project, I designed a unique character that blends elements of a robot and an alien, creating a paranormal-inspired creature. The character is predominantly green, adding an otherworldly and eerie aesthetic to the animation.
